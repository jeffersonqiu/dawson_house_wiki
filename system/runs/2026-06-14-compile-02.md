## Process notes / learnings for future Compiler runs

- **Free-text answers during review can overturn an entire proposal's premise.** Proposal 2
  looked like a routine Kitchen update until a free-text correction reframed nearly all of its
  content as belonging to a different (not-yet-existing) room. When that happens: pause, ask
  targeted confirming questions, do grounding research (read the affected notes + grep the raw
  sheet for concrete numbers) before restructuring — don't compile the reframe on the fly from
  the correction alone.
- **Review-queue proposal files are a durable audit trail for reframes, not just approvals.**
  Rather than silently compiling the corrected version, both affected proposal files
  (`...kitchen-design-updates.md` and `...bathroom-design.md`) were rewritten with a
  "Resolution" section up top (original text preserved below, marked "superseded") — so anyone
  reading the proposal later sees both what was originally proposed AND what was actually
  decided/why.
- **Renaming an item note** (Table Lamps (Kave) → Table Lamp (Kave Malachi)) is a write +
  delete, not an in-place rename — remember to update every `[[wikilink]]` reference in the
  owning room note's Items list afterward.
