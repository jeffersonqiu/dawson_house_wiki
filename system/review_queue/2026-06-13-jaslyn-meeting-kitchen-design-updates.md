## Open questions / judgment calls

1. **Travertine countertop + round undermount sink directly conflicts with the contracted
   quartz countertop** ("Lian Hin quartz Raw Edition Tundra", Appendix A Section K item 3,
   $2,035, itself marked "NEWLY EDITED" — i.e. recently finalised). A switch from quartz to
   travertine would be a material scope/cost change requiring Bud Studio's Change Order
   process (Renovation Agreement clause 4). I'm proposing this as a TASK rather than updating
   the Kitchen.md "Items"/scope figures, since I can't tell from the transcript alone whether
   this is a firm decision to switch or an option still being explored. **Human: please
   confirm whether travertine is a real swap-in or just one option discussed.**
2. **Wall colour items may actually belong to the Bathroom, not Kitchen** — the transcript's
   first outline section is literally titled "Design Decisions for Kitchen **and Bathroom**",
   and Speaker 5 separately notes "the three pictures almost look like two different rooms"
   (a floor-tile mismatch concern). I've placed the wall-colour/subway-tile/lime-wash items
   under Kitchen because they're discussed alongside the countertop/sink (clearly kitchen,
   per the later "Finalizing Design Details for Kitchen" section), but if this is wrong, these
   bullets should move to `system/review_queue/2026-06-13-jaslyn-meeting-bathroom-design.md`
   instead.
3. **Sink size (80cm)** — no current compiled spec to compare against (Kitchen.md doesn't
   record a sink size; the Reno Sheet's Master/Common Bathroom sink rows are separate $0.00
   placeholders, unrelated to this kitchen washing-counter sink). Treating this as a new
   confirm-able spec, not a change from an existing figure.
4. **Cabinet colour "creamier or light pink"** — Kitchen.md's design principles mention
   "BRIGHT, two-tone cabinet... cool Japandi" and previously considered "all-dark-brown
   finish" (instagram inspiration, apparently not adopted). This meeting's cabinet-colour
   discussion ("darker bottom half", avoid contrast with "red hair material") reads as a
   continuation/refinement of that open colour conversation, not a new topic — flagged as a
   task to track the render update through to a final colour decision.
